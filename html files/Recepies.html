How to Make Japanese Fried Rice (Yakimeshi)

Japanese fried rice, known as "Yakimeshi" (焼き飯), is a simple and flavorful dish. Using day-old rice is ideal to achieve the best texture. Here's how you can make it:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cooked rice (preferably chilled, day-old rice works best)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil (or sesame oil for a nuttier flavor)
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions (optional)
  • 1/2 cup vegetables (like carrots, peas, and corn)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2-3 tbsp soy sauce (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Optional: 1/2 cup protein (like diced chicken, shrimp, or tofu)

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Ingredients:

If you are using fresh rice, spread it out on a baking sheet and let it cool to remove moisture. Prepare and dice all your ingredients beforehand.

2. Heat the Pan:

Heat a large frying pan or wok over medium-high heat and add 1 tablespoon of oil.

3. Cook the Egg:

Pour in the beaten eggs and scramble quickly until just set. Remove the egg from the pan and set aside.

4. Stir-fry the Vegetables:

Add the remaining oil to the pan. Add the diced onion and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for another 2-3 minutes until tender.

5. Add Protein (Optional):

If using, add diced chicken, shrimp, or tofu, and cook until done. This step is optional if you prefer a vegetarian version.

6. Add Rice:

Break up any clumps of rice with your hands, then add the rice to the pan. Stir everything well to combine.

7. Season the Rice:

Add soy sauce evenly over the rice and stir. You can add more or less soy sauce according to your taste. Add salt and pepper to taste.

8. Combine Egg and Final Stir-fry:

Return the cooked egg to the pan and mix everything together. Add chopped green onions if using and stir for an additional 1-2 minutes.

9. Serve:

Serve hot as a main dish or a side.

Tips:

Day-old rice works best for fried rice as it has less moisture, making it easier to fry without becoming mushy. Feel free to customize with other sauces or seasonings like sesame oil, mirin, or a touch of oyster sauce for added depth of flavor. Enjoy your homemade Japanese fried rice!
